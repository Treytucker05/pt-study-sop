# Shoulder Complex Assessment & Examination Guide

This sheet serves as a standardized reference for the assessment of the shoulder (Glenohumeral, Acromioclavicular, Sternoclavicular, and Scapulothoracic joints).

---

## 1. Joint Mechanics & Arthrokinematics
*Reference: Magee, Neumann, Kisner & Colby*

### Glenohumeral (GH) Joint
*   **Resting (Open-Packed) Position:** 55° semi-abduction, 30° horizontal adduction (scapular plane).
*   **Closed-Packed Position:** Full abduction and lateral (external) rotation.
*   **Capsular Pattern:** Lateral Rotation > Abduction > Medial Rotation.
*   **End-Feels:**
    *   **Firm (Capsular/Ligamentous):** Flexion, Extension, Abduction, Medial/Lateral Rotation.
    *   **Soft (Tissue Approximation):** Horizontal Adduction, Adduction.

### GH Arthrokinematic Glides (Convex-on-Concave)
| Osteokinematic Motion | Arthrokinematic Glide (Humeral Head) | Roll |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flexion** | Posterior / Inferior spin | Superior |
| **Extension** | Anterior / Superior spin | Inferior |
| **Abduction** | **Inferior (Caudal)** | Superior |
| **Adduction** | Superior | Inferior |
| **External (Lateral) Rotation** | **Anterior** | Posterior |
| **Internal (Medial) Rotation** | **Posterior** | Anterior |

## 2. Range of Motion (ROM)
*Reference: AAOS / Norkin & White*

| Motion | Normal ROM | Fulcrum (Axis) | Proximal Arm | Distal Arm |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flexion** | 0 - 180° | Lateral aspect of greater tubercle | Midaxillary line of thorax | Lateral midline of humerus |
| **Extension** | 0 - 60° | Lateral aspect of greater tubercle | Midaxillary line of thorax | Lateral midline of humerus |
| **Abduction** | 0 - 180° | Anterior aspect of acromion | Parallel to anterior sternum | Anterior midline of humerus |
| **Internal Rotation** | 0 - 70° | Olecranon process | Perpendicular to floor | Ulnar styloid process |
| **External Rotation** | 0 - 90° | Olecranon process | Perpendicular to floor | Ulnar styloid process |

## 3. Muscle Length Testing (MLT)
*Reference: Kendall*

*   **Pectoralis Major (Clavicular):** Supine. Arm abducted to 90°, externally rotated. *Normal:* Arm rests flat on table.
*   **Pectoralis Major (Sternal):** Supine. Arm abducted to 135°, externally rotated. *Normal:* Arm rests flat on table.
*   **Pectoralis Minor:** Supine. Observe shoulder rest. *Tightness:* Anterior tilt/protraction of scapula; shoulder doesn't touch table.
*   **Latissimus Dorsi:** Supine, knees bent, back flat. Full shoulder flexion/abduction. *Tightness:* Inability to lay arms flat on table without arching lower back.

---

## 4. Manual Muscle Testing (MMT)
*Reference: Kendall / Daniels & Worthingham*

*   **Anterior Deltoid:** Seated. Arm flexed to 90° in slight horizontal adduction/internal rotation. Resist downward.
*   **Middle Deltoid / Supraspinatus:** Seated. Arm abducted to 90° in neutral rotation. Resist downward.
*   **Posterior Deltoid:** Prone. Arm abducted to 90°, horizontally abducted. Resist downward/forward.
*   **Infraspinatus & Teres Minor (ER):** Prone. Shoulder abducted 90°, elbow flexed 90°. Resist toward internal rotation (upward).
*   **Subscapularis (IR):** Prone. Shoulder abducted 90°, elbow flexed 90°. Resist toward external rotation (upward).
*   **Pectoralis Major (Upper):** Supine. Flex 90°, slight horizontal add/IR. Resist out and down.

## 5. Orthopedic Special Tests
*Reference: Magee / Orthobullets*

### Impingement
*   **Neer Test:** Forced passive flexion with IR. (Pain = positive for subacromial impingement).
*   **Hawkins-Kennedy:** 90° flexion, elbow 90°, forced internal rotation. (Pain = positive).

### Rotator Cuff Integrity
*   **Empty Can (Jobe):** 90° scaption, full IR (thumb down). Resist abduction. (Weakness/Pain = Supraspinatus).
*   **Drop Arm Test:** Abducted to 90°, patient lowers slowly. (Arm dropping/pain = Full thickness tear).

### Instability
*   **Apprehension Test:** Supine. 90° abduction, 90° ER. (Apprehension = Anterior instability).
*   **Relocation (Jobe):** Posterior pressure on humeral head during Apprehension. (Reduction in symptoms = positive).

### Labrum
*   **O'Brien's Test:** 90° flexion, 10° horizontal adduction, max IR. Resist downward. Repeat with ER. (Pain with IR relieved by ER = SLAP lesion).
