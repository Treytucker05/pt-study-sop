## Summary

Restore Trey’s Study System to its intended 3-part product identity and turn it into a premium individual product that can later scale without losing the original vision.

The product contract for this track is:

- **Brain** = learner-model engine
- **Scholar** = direct research partner
- **Tutor** = live protocol-led teacher

This track exists because the repo currently contains the right system in fragments, but the active canon, runtime wiring, UI framing, and commercialization surfaces do not all describe or support the same product.

## Locked Product Decisions

- Product direction is **premium individual first**, not institution-first.
- Archive and stale docs count as restoration evidence only when the same capability appears repeatedly and matches current owner intent.
- Brain is responsible for full study telemetry, learner-profile claims, fit signals, and visible hybrid archetypes.
- Scholar is learner-facing first and can ask the learner focused research/calibration questions.
- Scholar must be able to perform cited web research.
- Scholar does **not** teach course content; Tutor retains that role.
- Tutor remains chain-bound and SOP-bound.
- Brain must never directly steer Tutor pedagogy.
- Any Tutor adaptation must pass through a **bounded Scholar strategy envelope** with explicit provenance.
- Learner archetypes are visible, challengeable, and calibration-driven, not manually overridden labels.

## Track Goals

- Freeze one durable Brain / Scholar / Tutor product contract across active docs.
- Ship a Brain learner-profile MVP with evidence, confidence, contradictions, and learner challenge/calibration flow.
- Ship a Scholar learner-facing research MVP with investigations, learner questions, cited findings, and uncertainty handling.
- Add a bounded Scholar-to-Tutor strategy path without weakening SOP authority.
- Reframe the shell and trust surfaces so the product feels premium, explainable, and commercially credible.

## Release Gates

- **Gate A:** active canon and track artifacts agree on the 3-part contract
- **Gate B:** Brain ontology, evidence ownership, and migration rules are frozen
- **Gate C:** Scholar research runtime contract is frozen and supports cited investigations
- **Gate D:** Tutor strategy envelope is frozen and bounded by SOP
- **Gate E:** premium individual core loop works end to end
- **Gate F:** explainability, export/privacy, and value instrumentation are strong enough to sell

- broad multi-user tenancy work

- full redesign of every supporting page before Brain and Scholar MVPs are stable
