# Tutor Study Pass Checklist

Use this checklist live while the learner studies.

Working rules:
- use the learner's real active class/materials
- test one stage at a time first
- after each stage, capture likes, dislikes, friction, and missing expectations
- only pull in support pages if they directly block Tutor study flow

## Stage 1 - Launch

### Goal
Confirm the learner can land on `/tutor`, understand what is available, open the right workflow, and start without confusion.

### Actions
1. Open `/tutor`
2. Review the launch surface without clicking anything yet
3. Identify the workflow you actually want to study
4. Try the intended open/start action
5. If needed, inspect the start/resume controls

### Expected output
- the correct workflow is visible
- the current status is understandable
- open/resume/start actions feel obvious
- no stale or unrelated workflow state steals focus

### Ask the learner
- What is the first thing your eye goes to?
- Is it obvious how to continue your work?
- What feels useful?
- What feels cluttered or confusing?
- What do you wish existed on this screen?

## Stage 2 - Priming

### Goal
Confirm the learner can select the real class/material scope, inspect source material, extract PRIME artifacts, and feel ready to hand off to Tutor.

### Actions
1. Open `Priming`
2. Confirm the class and materials in scope
3. Review the source viewer
4. Run `Extract PRIME` if needed
5. Review `Learning Objectives`, `Study Spine`, `Hierarchical Map`, `Summary`, and `Terms`
6. Review Tutor handoff notes/readiness

### Expected output
- setup is obvious and not overloaded
- source viewer is usable
- PRIME artifact outputs are readable
- handoff feels like a real closeout, not a guess

### Ask the learner
- What feels smooth versus annoying?
- Which artifact is most useful?
- Which artifact is weakest or ugliest?
- What feels missing in Priming?
- Would you actually want to use this stage before studying?

## Stage 3 - Tutor

### Goal
Confirm the live study workspace feels like a real teaching/study surface instead of a technical shell.

### Actions
1. Open `Tutor`
2. Start interacting with the live teaching flow
3. Watch the stage/stepper/runtime information
4. Use any note, save, or feedback controls that matter during study
5. Stay long enough to judge whether the flow supports actual learning

### Expected output
- live chat feels study-usable
- stage state is understandable
- runtime controls do not fight the study flow
- capture actions make sense when needed

### Ask the learner
- Does this feel like it is actually teaching you?
- What feels distracting or overbuilt?
- What information is missing while studying?
- What do you like enough to keep?
- What would make you stop using the Tutor stage?

## Stage 4 - Polish

### Goal
Confirm the learner can review and refine outputs after studying without the stage feeling like busywork.

### Actions
1. Open `Polish`
2. Review the staged notes/artifacts/feedback
3. Try any rewrite, summary, QA, or card-candidate affordances that matter
4. Decide whether the stage feels necessary or redundant

### Expected output
- review artifacts are visible and understandable
- actions feel connected to study outputs
- the stage justifies itself before publish

### Ask the learner
- Does this help or just add extra steps?
- Which controls would you actually use?
- What feels missing before you would trust this stage?
- What should move out of Polish entirely?

## Stage 5 - Final Sync

### Goal
Confirm the learner can understand and trust the final closeout flow.

### Actions
1. Open `Final Sync`
2. Review the publish targets and closeout information
3. Judge whether the stage makes sense before actually saving work out
4. If appropriate, inspect the note/card closeout affordances

### Expected output
- closeout state is visible
- the learner understands what will be saved where
- retry/failure trust feels plausible

### Ask the learner
- Do you trust this stage?
- Is it clear what goes to Obsidian versus Anki versus Brain?
- What feels risky, unclear, or unnecessary?

## Stage 6 - Full End-to-End Pass

### Goal
Verify the full flow still makes sense in sequence, not just as isolated stages.

### Actions
1. Start at `Launch`
2. Move through the workflow naturally using the real active class/materials
3. Do not optimize or skip around unless the product forces you to
4. Record where momentum breaks

### Expected output
- stage transitions feel coherent
- no stage feels like a dead-end or detour
- the overall workflow supports a real study session

### Ask the learner
- Where did momentum drop?
- Which stage felt best?
- Which stage felt worst?
- What are the top three things you would change first?
